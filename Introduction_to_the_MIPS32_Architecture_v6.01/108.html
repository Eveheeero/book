A list of FPU-approximate arithmetic operations is given in Table 7.6. 7.2.2.3 FPU Multiply-add Instructions Four compound-operation instructions perform variations of multiply-accumulate—that is, multiply two operands, accumulate the result to a third operand, and produce a result. These instructions are listed in Table 7.7. Release 6 removes these instructions and replaces them by fused multiply-add instructions, listed in Table 7.8. Arithmetic and rounding behavior: The product is rounded according to the current rounding mode prior to the accumulation. The accumulated result is also rounded. This model meets the IEEE-754-1985 accuracy specification; the result is numerically identical to an equivalent computation using a sequence of multiply, add/subtract, or negate instructions. Similarly, exceptions and flags behave as if the operation was implemented with a sequence of multiply, add/subtract and negate instructions. This behavior is often known as “Non-Fused”.
