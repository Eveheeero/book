These are signed ver- sions of the following operations: • Add • Subtract • Multiply • Divide The add and subtract operations labeled “unsigned” are actually modulo arithmetic without overflow detection. The “signed” add and subtract instructions detect overflow beyond the limits of a signed 2’s complement integer. There are also unsigned versions of multiply and divide, as well as a full complement of shift and logical operations. Logical operations are not sensitive to the width of the register. microMIPS32 provided 32-bit integers and 32-bit arithmetic. MIPS64 adds 64-bit integers and provides separate arithmetic and shift instructions for 64-bit operands. 5.2.1 ALU Immediate and Three-Operand Instructions Table 5.8 lists those arithmetic and logical instructions that operate on one operand from a register and the other from a 16-bit immediate value supplied by the instruction word.
