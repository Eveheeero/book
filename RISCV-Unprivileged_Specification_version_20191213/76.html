Thus, accesses to CSRs other than the aforementioned three can be freely reordered with respect to FENCE instructions without violating this speciﬁcation. For CSR accesses that cause side eﬀects, the above ordering constraints apply to the order of the initiation of those side eﬀects but does not necessarily apply to the order of the completion of those side eﬀects. The hardware platform may deﬁne that accesses to certain CSRs are strongly ordered, as deﬁned by the Memory-Ordering PMAs section in Volume II of this manual. Accesses to strongly ordered CSRs have stronger ordering constraints with respect to accesses to both weakly ordered CSRs and accesses to memory-mapped I/O regions.
