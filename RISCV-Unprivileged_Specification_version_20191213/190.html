In this case, even though the AMOSWAP does not enforce ordering with an aq bit, the fence nevertheless enforces that the acquire AMOSWAP appears earlier in the global memory order than all loads and stores in the critical section. Note, however, that in this case, the fence also enforces additional orderings: it also requires that the “Arbitrary unrelated load” at the start of the program appears earlier in the global memory order than the loads and stores of the critical section. (This particular fence does not, however, enforce any ordering with respect to the “Arbitrary unrelated store” at the start of the snippet.) In this way, fence-enforced orderings are slightly coarser than orderings enforced by .aq.
