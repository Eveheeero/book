B-108 Vol. 2D INSTRUCTION FORMATS AND ENCODINGS B.17 FLOATING-POINT INSTRUCTION FORMATS AND ENCODINGS Table B-38 shows the five different formats used for floating-point instructions. In all cases, instructions are at least two bytes long and begin with the bit pattern 11011. The Mod and R/M fields of the ModR/M byte have the same interpretation as the corresponding fields of the integer instructions. The SIB byte and disp (displacement) are optionally present in instructions that have Mod and R/M fields. Their presence depends on the values of Mod and R/M, as for integer instructions.
