ARM Instructions A4-136 Copyright Â© 1996-1998, 2000, 2004, 2005 ARM Limited. All rights reserved. ARM DDI 0100I Usage Use SHSUB16 to speed up operations on arrays of halfword data. This is similar to the way you can use SADD16. See the usage subsection for SADD16 on page A4-119 for details. You can also use SHSUB16 for operations on complex numbers that are held as pairs of 16-bit integers or Q15 numbers. If you hold the real and imaginary parts of a complex number in the bottom and top half of a register respectively, then the instruction: SHSUB16 Rd, Ra, Rb performs the complex arithmetic operation Rd = (Ra - Rb)/2. SHSUB16 does not set any flags, as overflow is not possible. Notes Use of R15 Specifying R15 for register <Rd>, <Rm>, or <Rn> has UNPREDICTABLE results.
