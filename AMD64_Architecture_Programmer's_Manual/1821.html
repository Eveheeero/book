554 Opcode and Operand Encodings AMD64 Technology 24594—Rev. 3.35—June 2023 A.2.2 SIB Operand References Figure A-3 on page 555 shows the format of a scale-index-base (SIB) byte. Some instructions have an SIB byte following their ModRM byte to define memory addressing for the complex-addressing modes described in “Effective Addresses” in APM Volume 1. The SIB byte has three fields—scale, index, and base—that define the scale factor, index-register number, and base-register number for 32- bit and 64-bit complex addressing modes. In 64-bit mode, the REX.B and REX.X bits extend the encoding of the SIB byte’s base and index fields.
