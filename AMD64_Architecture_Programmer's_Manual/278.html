In 3D graphics applications, for example, objects are typically represented by triangles, each of whose vertices are located in 3D space by a matrix of coordinate values, and matrix transforms are performed to simulate object movement. The 64-bit media integer and floating-point instructions can perform several types of matrix-vector or matrix-matrix operations, such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and accumulation.
