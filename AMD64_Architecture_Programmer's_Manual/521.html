XSS Register 3.2.9 Speculation Control MSRs Modern processors implement hardware techniques such as branch prediction, speculative execution and out-of-order processing to significantly improve performance. If the processor incorrectly predicts or speculates on an outcome, this is detected and any speculative results are discarded. The processor then supplies the architecturally correct, in-order response to the program's instructions. Even though the speculative results are discarded, microarchitectural side effects may remain which can be detected by software, and which in some cases may lead to side-channel vulnerabilities. The two speculation control MSRs, SPEC_CTRL (MSR 048h) and PRED_CMD (MSR 049h), enable hardware features that are designed to limit certain types of speculation.
