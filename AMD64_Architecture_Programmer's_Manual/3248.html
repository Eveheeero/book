250 FICOMx x87 Floating-Point Instruction Reference AMD64 Technology 26569—Rev. 3.16—November 2021 Converts a 16-bit or 32-bit signed integer value to double-extended-precision format, compares it to the value in ST(0), and sets the C0, C2, and C3 condition code flags in the x87 status word to reflect the results. The comparison operation ignores the sign of zero (–0.0 = +0.0). After performing the comparison operation, the FICOMP instruction pops the x87 register stack. If ST(0) is a NaN or is in an unsupported format, the instruction sets the condition flags to “unordered.” Related Instructions FCOM, FCOMPP, FCOMI, FCOMIP, FTST, FUCOMI, FUCOMIP, FXAM rFLAGS Affected None FICOM FICOMP Floating-Point Integer Compare Mnemonic Opcode Description FICOM mem16int DE /2 Convert the contents of mem16int to double-extended- precision format, compare the result to the contents of ST(0), and set condition flags to reflect the results of the comparison. FICOM mem32int DA /2 Convert the contents of mem32int to double-extended- precision format, compare the result to the contents of ST(0), and set condition flags to reflect the results of the comparison. FICOMP mem16int DE /3 Convert the contents of mem16int to double-extended- precision format, compare the result to the contents of ST(0), set condition flags to reflect the results of the comparison, and pop the x87 register stack. FICOMP mem32int DA /3 Convert the contents of mem32int to double-extended- precision format, compare the result to the contents of ST(0), set condition flags to reflect the results of the comparison, and pop the x87 register stack.
