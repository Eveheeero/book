Relationship between the LDT and GDT 4.6.4 Local Descriptor-Table Register The local descriptor-table register (LDTR) points to the location of the LDT in memory, defines its size, and specifies its attributes. The LDTR has two portions. A visible portion holds the LDT selector, and a hidden portion holds the LDT descriptor. When the LDT selector is loaded into the LDTR, the processor automatically loads the LDT descriptor from the GDT into the hidden portion of the LDTR. The LDTR is loaded in one of two ways: • Using the LLDT instruction (see “LLDT and LTR Instructions” on page 180). • Performing a task switch (see “Switching Tasks” on page 379).
