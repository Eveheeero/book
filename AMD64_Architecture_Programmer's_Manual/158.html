Saturating (also called limiting or clamping) instructions limit the value of a result to the maximum or minimum value representable by the applicable data type. Saturating versions of integer vector-arithmetic instructions operate on byte-sized and word-sized elements. These instructions—for example, (V)PACKx, (V)PADDSx, (V)PADDUSx, (V)PSUBSx, and (V)PSUBUSx—saturate signed or unsigned data at the vector-element level when the element reaches its maximum or minimum representable value. Saturation avoids overflow or underflow errors. Many of the integer multiply and accumulate instructions saturate the cumulative results of the multiplication and addition (accumulation) operations before writing the final results to the destination (accumulator) register. Note, however, that not all multiply and accumulate instructions saturate results. The examples in Table 4-2 below illustrate saturating and non-saturating results with word operands. Saturation for other data-type sizes follows similar rules. Once saturated, the saturated value is treated like any other value of its type.
