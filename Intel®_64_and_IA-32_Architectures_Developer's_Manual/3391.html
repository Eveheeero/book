Vol. 3A 11-7 ADVANCED PROGRAMMABLE INTERRUPT CONTROLLER (APIC) 11.4.2 Presence of the Local APIC Beginning with the P6 family processors, the presence or absence of an on-chip local APIC can be detected using the CPUID instruction. When the CPUID instruction is executed with a source operand of 1 in the EAX register, bit 9 of the CPUID feature flags returned in the EDX register indicates the presence (set) or absence (clear) of a local APIC. 11.4.3 Enabling or Disabling the Local APIC The local APIC can be enabled or disabled in either of two ways: 1.
